Chole Bhature Recipe

Description

Chole Bhature is a classic North Indian dish featuring spicy, flavorful chickpea curry (chole) served with fluffy, deep-fried bread (bhature). This hearty combination offers a satisfying blend of rich spices and textures, perfect for a delicious and filling meal.

Ingredients

  1. For Chole:
    1. 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight)
    2. 1 large onion (finely chopped)
    3. 2 tomatoes (pureed)
    4. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    5. 1 tablespoon chole masala (or garam masala)
    6. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    7. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    8. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    9. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    10. 2 green chilies (slit)
    11. 1 tablespoon oil
    12. Salt to taste
    13. Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  2. For Bhature:
    1. 2 cups all-purpose flour
    2. 1/2 cup plain yogurt
    3. 1 teaspoon baking powder
    4. 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    5. 1/2 teaspoon sugar
    6. 1/2 teaspoon salt
    7. Warm water (as needed)
    8. Oil (for deep frying)

Recipe

  1. For Chole:
    1. Heat oil in a pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    2. Add finely chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
    3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
    4. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates.
    5. Add chole masala, turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
    6. Add soaked and drained chickpeas. Pour in enough water to cover the chickpeas.
    7. Simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until chickpeas are tender. Adjust seasoning as needed.
    8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
  2. For Bhature:
    1. In a large bowl, mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
    2. Add yogurt and mix until the dough starts to come together.
    3. Knead the dough with warm water until smooth and elastic. Cover and let it rest for at least 2 hours.
    4. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a round, slightly thick disc.
    5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry each disc until puffed and golden brown on both sides.
    6. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with the chole.
