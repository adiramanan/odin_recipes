Dosa Recipe

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish consisting of thin, crispy crepes made from fermented rice and lentil batter. Often served with coconut chutney and sambar, it’s a delightful, savory meal that’s both light and satisfying.

Ingredients

  1. For Dosa Batter:
    1. 1 cup rice
    2. 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
    3. 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
    4. 1/2 teaspoon salt
    5. Water (as needed)
  2. For Serving:
    1. Coconut chutney
    2. Sambar

Recipe

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain and grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter, adding water as needed to achieve a pourable consistency.
  3. Transfer the batter to a bowl, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place for 12-24 hours until it rises and becomes slightly bubbly.
  4. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet. Pour a ladle of batter onto the skillet and spread it in a thin, circular motion.
  5. Drizzle a little oil around the edges. Cook until the dosa is golden brown and crispy, then flip and cook briefly on the other side.
  6. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
