Samosa Recipe

Description

Samosa is a beloved Indian snack featuring crispy, golden pastry filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. These triangle-shaped delights are perfect for snacking or as appetizers.

Ingredients

  1. For Filling:
    1. 2 large potatoes (boiled and mashed)
    2. 1/2 cup peas (boiled)
    3. 1 tablespoon oil
    4. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    5. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    6. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    7. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    8. 1 teaspoon garam masala
    9. 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
    10. 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    11. Salt to taste
    12. Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  2. For Pastry:
    1. 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    2. 1/4 cup oil or ghee
    3. 1/2 teaspoon caraway seeds (optional)
    4. 1/2 teaspoon salt
    5. Water (as needed)
  3. Oil (for deep frying)

Recipe

  1. For the filling, heat oil in a pan. Add cumin and mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  2. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Stir for a minute.
  3. Add mashed potatoes and peas, mixing well. Cook for 5-7 minutes, then add lemon juice and chopped coriander leaves. Set aside to cool.
  4. For the pastry, mix flour, salt, and caraway seeds (if using) in a bowl. Add oil or ghee and rub in until crumbly.
  5. Add water gradually and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into thin circles. Cut each circle in half to form semi-circles.
  7. Fold each semi-circle into a cone shape, sealing the edge with water. Fill with the prepared filling and seal the open edge to form a triangle.
  8. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the samosas until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  9. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.
