Chilli Paneer Recipe

Description

Chili Paneer is a tantalizing Indo-Chinese dish featuring crispy paneer cubes stir-fried with vibrant bell peppers, onions, and chilies. Tossed in a savory-sweet sauce with soy, garlic, and a hint of spice, this flavorful, aromatic dish is a perfect blend of heat and sweetness. Ideal for a satisfying appetizer or main course.

Ingredients

  1. Paneer (cubed)
  2. Bell peppers (diced)
  3. Green chilies (sliced)
  4. Soy sauce
  5. Tomato ketchup
  6. Garlic (minced)
  7. Ginger (minced)
  8. Cornstarch
  9. Vegetable oil
  10. Salt
  11. Black pepper
  12. Paneer (cubed)
  13. Paneer (cubed)

Recipe

  1. In a bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour. Coat the paneer cubes with this mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the coated paneer cubes until golden and crispy. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add a bit more oil if needed. Saut√© 2 cloves of minced garlic and 1 tablespoon of minced ginger until fragrant.
  4. Add 1 chopped onion, 1 chopped bell pepper (red or green), and 2 sliced green chilies to the pan. Stir-fry until vegetables are slightly tender.
  5. In a small bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 2 tablespoons of tomato ketchup, and 1 tablespoon of vinegar (optional). Add this sauce mixture to the pan.
  6. Add the fried paneer cubes back into the pan. Toss everything together until well coated with the sauce. Adjust salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  8. Garnish with chopped spring onions (optional) and serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.
